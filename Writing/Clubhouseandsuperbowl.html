Clubhouse & Super Bowl

Tony Wang



Alright, let’s get started.

First off, I’m writing in English simply because I can have a better flow. Chinese is my mother tongue but I could not be as efficient with the words – would always try to portray too much and sound more well-rounded in a way. Since this article is mainly observational & informational, I would try to be more concise to capture what I thought about today, in English.

Secondly, clubhouse. It’s one of the things that pops up and everyone’s asking about it. I was not a fan, still am not – but I did spend around 1 hour to listen to a chatroom regarding the topic of how Chinese people in the US think about going back to China to live. I will list my observations and thoughts as bullet lists below.

Observations:

  • There is a moderator in each room – kind of solved the issue of people overlapping, especially during some heated topics when everyone wants to talk.
  • Users seem to be high-end. Educated at least. Profile completion rate is high.
  • Room seems to be long existed. The room is still open when I check in the afternoon, several hours later.

Thoughts:

  • I should read more books to avoid covering topics outside of my reach. Some people are not making much sense when they speak, and I think that stems from a lack of knowledge and information inputs.
  • Only a small fraction of the total time spent is useful – I’m trying to listen to people with similar backgrounds sharing their thoughts but found few of them. My target is not clear going in.
  • The app could be a useful tool when you have more time, or nothing better to do (driving). Put it on as an educational/informational podcast (a high-end room in Chinese) would be of much better assimilation.
